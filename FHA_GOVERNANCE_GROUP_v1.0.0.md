# 1. Purpose of the FHA

The FHA exists to:

1. Protect the Human-Interpretive layer of the DGCP protocol  
2. Determine whether a proof reflects *Real Work*  
3. Provide contextual judgment where machines cannot  
4. Preserve labor dignity and prevent misuse of data  
5. Resolve disputes and ensure fairness across all workers  

The FHA is the guardian of **Labor Justice**, not technical correctness.

# 3. FHA Membership Structure

FHA governance follows a **federated model**:

- **Local FHA Groups:** Review proofs within a region or industry  
- **Regional FHA Councils:** Resolve escalations  
- **Global FHA Council:** Handles multi-region disputes and rule updates  

No group has unilateral power. All must follow protocol rules.

# 5. FHA Boundaries (Strict Prohibitions)

To protect workers:

The FHA MUST NEVER:

- Require personal identity from workers  
- Demand exact GPS location  
- Force workers to use certain apps or tools  
- Penalize low-quality photos  
- Use AI-based classification  
- Evaluate efficiency or productivity  
- Compare workers against each other  

DGCP is **not** a worker surveillance tool.

# 8. Required Skills for FHA Members

Members must have:

- Contextual knowledge of the industry or region  
- Understanding of DGCP ethics  
- Ability to interpret human descriptions  
- Sensitivity to cultural and economic differences  
- Commitment to fairness and privacy  

No technical background required.

DGCP requires FHA members to undergo:

- Human-First Ethics Training

# 10. Accountability Mechanisms

To prevent abuse:

- All audit decisions are public  
- Members with repeated bias patterns face removal  
- Workers can appeal decisions  
- Regional and global councils provide oversight  
- Auditor identities are logged pseudonymously  

Bias is treated as a protocol violation.

# 11. Global Federation Rules

- Any country or NGO can host FHA nodes  
- No single nation can dominate governance  
- No military, intelligence, or corporate body may control decisions  
- FHA governance is built on **neutrality and decentralization**
